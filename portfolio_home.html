How I Work

Operating
Principles

01

Start with the problem behind the problem

Users describe symptoms, not root causes. Real product strategy starts with understanding the cognitive and workflow gaps that produce the symptoms — not with validating solutions people already have in mind. Discovery is a diagnostic exercise, not a feature poll.

02

Clarity is the deliverable

In complex, cross-functional environments, the PM's primary job is to create clarity: on the problem, on the priority, on the decision, on the trade-off. Ambiguity is expensive. I invest heavily in alignment upfront — through written briefs, decision logs, and explicit OKRs — so teams can execute without constant supervision.

03

Influence without authority through shared outcomes

Most meaningful healthcare product work crosses org lines. I align cross-functional teams not through hierarchy but through a shared north-star question: does this help a clinician make a faster, better-informed decision? That question replaces authority as the alignment mechanism.

04

Make trade-offs explicit, not implicit

Every roadmap decision has a cost. Surfacing what we're saying no to — and why — is as important as what we're building. I use structured prioritization (RICE, OKRs, decision principles) to make trade-offs deliberate rather than defaulted into.

05

Build for adoption, not just launch

The GTM motion is part of the product — not a handoff that happens after. I design adoption strategy, sales enablement, and customer rollout programs in parallel with the product itself. That's why the work scales instead of plateauing after launch.
